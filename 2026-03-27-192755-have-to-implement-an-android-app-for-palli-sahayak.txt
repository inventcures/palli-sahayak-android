Why your flow needs the spec phase                                              
Without a spec, Claude Code will make architectural choices you'll regret вҖ”     
it'll default to a reasonable-but-generic Android setup that may not match your 
 stack (e.g. it picks LiveData when you wanted StateFlow, or skips Hilt because 
 you didn't say DI). A 10-minute spec prompt saves 10 hours of refactoring.

     Plan: Palli Sahayak Android App вҖ” Detailed Specs & Codebase Integration

     Context

     Palli Sahayak is a voice-first AI clinical decision support tool for
     palliative care, currently deployed as a Python FastAPI backend (~30,000
     lines). It serves ASHA workers, family caregivers, and patients via
     telephone, WhatsApp, and web interfaces. The EVAH proposal (Pathway A, $1M,
      12 months) evaluates the system across 4 Indian clinical sites with ~200
     participants.

     Why this change: The system needs a native Android app to:
     1. Enable offline-first operation in rural/remote sites (CCF Coimbatore,
     CCHRC Silchar)
     2. Provide a dedicated voice-first mobile interface for ASHA home visits
     3. Collect evaluation data (SUS scores, time-motion, vignette crossover)
     4. Integrate with ABDM/ABHA for India's digital health ecosystem
     5. Support the 8-month active evaluation period across 4 sites

     What prompted it: The EVAH v4 proposal draft specifies deployment to ~200
     frontline users who need hands-free, offline-capable voice AI during home
     visits. Research on HCW apps (Simple App, CommCare, mSakhi) confirms native
      Android with offline-first architecture is the validated approach for
     Indian CHW deployment.
